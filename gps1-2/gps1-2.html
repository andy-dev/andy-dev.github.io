What is pair programming:
Pair programming is working with someone else or with group to discuss a problem, issue or challenge. Pair programming involves using everyones expertise to brainstorm and layout the best way to accomplish a defined task.

Why is it important?
It is important because it is always best to bring different perspective, ideas or feedback when working on a problem. As the saying goes, two heads are better than one.

Compare Driver vs Navigator.
There are multiple interpretations and for every pairing session it is important to define them before the session begins. Normally, the driver, similar to the car analogy, has control of the wheel steps on the gas deciding the initial path to take. The navigator acts most of the time as a copilot pointing out warnings and shortcuts in the road In coding challenge for example, the driver would start typing out and setting the skeleton of the code making andy modification necessary to get the code to work. Meanwhile the navigator would point out typos, syntax and/or research the web for documentation.

Tips:
It is important to know when to program and always set a goal on what it is you want to achieve in your session.
